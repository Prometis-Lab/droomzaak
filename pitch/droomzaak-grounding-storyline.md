## Concept in one line

A single hero is **transported through transforming environments by a narrator only she can hear**, while the world around her treats each impossible new setting as completely normal. Each environment is the proof of one thing Droomzaak unlocks — and the only thing standing between her and that dream is the **administratie & keuzestress** the product removes.

## The heart (read this first)

> Ondernemen begint met een droom — maar verdrinkt meestal in administratie en keuzestress. Deze film laat de droom *eerst* zien (speels, verrassend, levend), confronteert je dan met de chaos die hem tegenhoudt, en zet **Droomzaak** neer als de uitweg. **Da's een zaak van dromen.**

*(EN: Entrepreneurship starts with a dream — but usually drowns in paperwork and choice-stress. The film shows the dream first (playful, surprising, alive), then confronts you with the chaos that blocks it, and positions Droomzaak as the way through.)*

The tone is **confident, playful, a little surreal.** Pace is **brisk** — the transitions are the energy; momentum never stops.

---

## The core device (the whole effect)

The hero is **fourth-wall-aware**: she hears the narrator, reacts to the transitions, and is visibly *in on it*. Everyone and everything else stays committed to its own reality and never acknowledges the chaos. **That asymmetry is the effect** — the audience is let in on a private conversation between narrator and hero that the world can't perceive.

- **Tonal references:** Old Spice *"The Man Your Man Could Smell Like"* (seamless world-rebuilding, locked continuity) + a Deadpool-style knowing-wink aware protagonist (hero ↔ narrator dynamic).
- **The product is explained through what each environment *shows*, not a list of claims.** Each new world exists because of something Droomzaak does; the narrator names the beat, the environment becomes the proof. **At the resolution the product itself is shown directly** — a brief walkthrough of the real app — as the final proof, not just named.

## What NEVER changes (the invariant spine)

1. **The device** — fourth-wall-aware hero + narrator duet; the world stays committed to normalcy. Non-negotiable.
2. **The arc** — **boring reality → the dream made vivid (transforms) → back to reality → the chaos that blocks it (keuzestress) → Droomzaak as the way through (the product shown working) → CTA.**
3. **The narrator** — warm, confident **male Flemish (nl-BE)** voice; the only thing the hero can hear. *(Full duet script below — invariant.)*
4. **The hero is non-speaking** — she answers the narrator with looks, nods, reactions (knowing energy). All language lives in the narration + on-screen text.
5. **The transform mechanic** — environment + wardrobe rebuild around a **locked hero** (framing, eyeline, motion constant); every scene change lands **on a movement** (a pull-forward, turn, whip-pan, duck, hand-off).
6. **The tone** — confident, playful, surreal, brisk.
7. **The pain beat** — the **keuzestress tab-explosion**: a Google search spawns pop-up after pop-up until the screen drowns. This is the only "wall," and it's comedic, not heavy.
8. **The resolution** — the way out is **the product itself**: a brief walkthrough of the real Droomzaak app (describe your dream → it guides you through location, permits, finances), then it lands on the **Droomzaak landing page** with the CTA.
9. **The signature line** — *"Ondernemen, da's een zaak van dromen."* (pun: *zaak* = both 'business' and 'matter'). It returns as the **closing CTA payoff** — the spot ends on *"droomzaak.be, want ondernemen, da's een zaak van dromen."*, bookending Beat 6.

## The visual storyline (shooting script)

> Target runtime **~60s** (brand spot ~38s + product-demo tail ~22s; scales up with more dream modules). Each beat is one continuous, motion-matched move — except the product demo (Beat 9), which is a screen capture of the real app.

**Beat 0 · The grey desk** *(setup, no VO)*
Lore at a dull desk job — grey decor, grey clothes, tired computer, a stack of papers. She's bored. A big sigh; head sinks into her hand. *Another* stack of papers thuds onto the desk. Hold the dreariness for a beat.

**Beat 1 · The voice** — Narrator: *"Je job beu?"*
Lore frowns, glances around trying to place the voice, then looks **straight at camera** — the fourth wall opens. *(The device is established here.)*

**Beat 2 · The question** — Narrator: *"Was dit je droomjob?"*
She shakes her head, a flicker of disappointment.

**Beat 3 · The pull (Transform → bistro)** — Narrator: *"Wat dan wel?"*
Lore is **pulled forward into the next frame** (Old Spice-style: hero constant, world rebuilds around her). The grey desk swaps to a **trendy bistro**; her grey clothes resolve into a **colourful outfit + apron**. Camera eases back to reveal **her own bistro**, a coffee machine blowing steam beside her. She smiles, surprised. *She's aware of the transition; the bistro is not.*

**Beat 4 · Option A (Transform → kitchen)** — Narrator: *"Dit meer je stijl?"*
On a movement, the world rebuilds again into a **professional kitchen**; Lore is now in **chef's whites**, looking around in amazement.

**Beat 5 · Option B** — Narrator: *"Of dit?"*
Lore **nods at camera**, a natural, happy smile. *(Add more `{{DREAM_ENVIRONMENTS}}` modules here to extend.)*

**Beat 6 · Back to reality** — Narrator: *"Ondernemen, da's een zaak van dromen."*
Smooth fade back to the **original desk shot** — Lore behind her grey desk again, but the look in her eyes has changed.

**Beat 7 · The keuzestress** *(no VO — let it play)*
Cut to **over-the-shoulder** on her screen. Google is open: **"Hoe start ik een restaurant?"** A new tab pops open. Then another. Then more and more in fast succession until the screen **drowns in pop-ups**. Camera **pushes in** toward the screen.

**Beat 8 · The way out** — Narrator: *"Laat administratie en keuzestress je dromen niet tegenhouden."*
The push-in transitions smoothly into a clean **screen capture**, which **fades to black**, then resolves on the **Droomzaak landing page**.

**Beat 9 · The product** *(NEW — show the app working)* — Narrator: *"Het enige dat je moet doen is je droomzaak beschrijven, en wij loodsen je … tot wat je kan verwachten op financieel vlak. Zodat jij kan blijven dromen."*
A brief, brisk walkthrough of the **real Droomzaak product**: describe-your-dream input → the Droomkaart and data-source insights → location, permits, and financial expectations → the *Droomzaak-pakket*. **This is the beat we added** because the dream-transforms alone weren't **showing the product itself** enough. *(The Lore fourth-wall device pauses here — it's the product on screen, not the hero. Keep it tight so it reads as proof, not a manual.)*

**Beat 10 · The brand / CTA** — Narrator: *"Bezoek onze website en laat ons je helpen je droomleven waar te maken. droomzaak.be, want ondernemen, da's een zaak van dromen."*
Back to the landing page → **logo end card** (`droomzaak.png`) on warm earth tone, tagline *"Van droom naar zaak."* The signature line returns as the closing payoff — the only fully-still moment.

## Visual mechanics (invariant principles)

- **Lock the hero across environments** — framing, eyeline, and motion stay constant so transitions read as seamless. She is the only constant; backgrounds + any supporting cast fully transform.
- **Cut on movement** — every environment change happens on a pull-forward, turn, whip-pan, duck, or hand-off. The motion hides the seam.
- **AI-generation method (our path):** lock a **Lore character reference** and reuse it in every shot. Engineer each match-cut with **start-frame / end-frame control** (Runway Gen-4.5, Kling 3.0): the *end pose* of one environment = the *start pose* of the next, same framing — the model rebuilds the world around the held pose. This is the AI-native equivalent of Old Spice's in-camera swaps. *(Live-action practical/post-stitched is the fallback if a hybrid shoot is ever budgeted.)*

## Performance direction (invariant)

- **Hero (Lore):** reacts to the narrator, anticipates the transitions, plays the *"I know what's happening, you don't"* energy. Surprise → delight → knowing confidence.
- **Supporting cast / world:** never break, never react to her awareness — **total commitment to normalcy.** The asymmetry is the joke.

---

## How to make a new version (casting checklist)

1. Choose `{{HERO}}`, theme `{{DESK_WORLD}}`, pick the `{{DREAM_ENVIRONMENTS}}` (1–3 modules), and write the matching `{{GOOGLE_QUERY}}`.
2. **Do not touch** the duet narration, the device, the arc, or the tone.
3. Regenerate only the transform shots + the Google query; reuse desk, keuzestress mechanic, and end card.
4. The Flemish narration and music never need re-rendering between versions.

## Guardrails (so it stays sharp, not corny)

- Playful and confident — **never** smug or random. Every transform demonstrates a real benefit (a real dream Droomzaak can plan).
- The fourth-wall device must stay *clean*: only Lore is aware; the world is dead committed to normalcy.
- Brisk pace — if a beat doesn't add momentum or proof, cut it.
- Keep the keuzestress beat comedic and fast; don't let it turn into a heavy "wall of doubt."
- The **product-demo beat (Beat 9)** is the one place the fourth-wall device pauses — it's the literal app as proof. Keep it brisk and concrete; if it sprawls into a feature tour it breaks the spot's momentum. It earns its length only by *showing the product actually working*.
- Brand: warm but with energy; the end card is the only fully-still moment. (See `dream-narrative-style` for tone law; `frontend-design` for anti-AI-slop on the end card.)
